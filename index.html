01

Manufacturing Cost Intelligence System

Margin forecasting and what-if analysis for manufacturing lines.

  • Problem: Operations needed a way to compare scenarios under changing energy, labor, and material costs without relying on gut feel.
  • What I built: A model that ingests cost drivers and demand assumptions, runs scenarios, and outputs projected margins and sensitivity.
  • Stack: Python, pandas, simulation logic, Excel / BI front-end.
  • Impact: Makes it obvious how margin shifts when one driver moves, instead of hiding assumptions in spreadsheets.

Process engineering

How I think about processes

The short version: map the flow, instrument it, then design the smallest system that actually changes behavior.

01 · Map

Start with the real flow: materials, information, decisions. Swimlanes, hand-offs, constraints. No buzzwords, just “what actually happens?”.

02 · Measure

Pick a few metrics that matter (lead time, service level, margin, failure rate) and wire data around them. If we can’t measure it, we can’t argue about it properly.

03 · Model

Use models to explore scenarios and trade-offs: routing choices, inventory policies, production plans. The goal is insight, not a black box.

04 · Iterate

Push small changes back into the process, monitor impact, and refine. This is where the engineering mindset meets day-to-day operations.
