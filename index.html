Selected Projects

A small set of projects that solve real problems. Everything here either ships, runs on data, or both.

Manufacturing Cost Intelligence System

Margin forecasting and what-if analysis for manufacturing lines.

  • Problem: Operations needed a way to compare scenarios under changing energy, labor, and material costs without guessing on margins.
  • What I built: A model that ingests cost drivers and demand assumptions, runs scenarios, and outputs projected margins and risk bands.
  • Stack: Python, pandas, basic simulation logic, Excel / Power BI for the front-end.
  • Outcome: Makes it obvious how margin shifts when one cost driver moves, instead of burying assumptions in spreadsheets.

Logistics / Route & Inventory Analysis

Data view on routes, inventory levels, and transportation costs.

  • Problem: Fragmented view of stock across locations and carriers made it hard to see where money and time were actually being burned.
  • What I built: Data model and dashboards that tie inventory, routes, and service levels into one picture.
  • Stack: Python, SQL, spreadsheets / BI tooling used at work.
  • Outcome: Faster answers to “where is the bottleneck?” and “what happens if we change this lane or policy?”.

About

Short version of how I ended up caring this much about logistics, data, and getting out of the “okay at everything” zone.

I’m a final-year Industrial Engineering student from Mexicali, currently working as a remote logistics analyst and building data-heavy projects around supply chains and economics.

I grew up as the younger brother following a very strong act – same school, same sports, same cities. For a long time I was “good enough” at everything, but never the one people pointed to. Over the last couple of years, that changed: I started doing real work in logistics, building systems around data instead of just using tools, and aiming for places that used to feel out of reach.

This site is mostly a record of that shift: what I build, the questions I’m chasing, and what I’m currently learning.
